Enjoy Amazing Experiences In Japan With Us

Book an amazing trip to Japan's top amusement parks and theme parks through our website

Design Thinking Process

Empathise

I have identified my website’s target audience to be family with children below 18 years old, living in Singapore. Once my target audience is defined, I conducted an online survey of 30 participants to collect quantitative data on their preferences, habits and pain points. Once I received their response, I organised and reviewed the information collected and summarised the research findings.

User needs: Users want to explore as many theme parks as they can in Japan. They want a website with all the necessary information on theme parks in Japan.

User pain points: Users have no idea how to start researching because there are too many theme parks in Japan. Users also wonder if there are any promotions when they book two or more theme parks.

Define

An empathy map was created to help one better understand the users’ needs, thoughts and feelings. This empathy map was used as a foundation to design the microsite, which allows me to effectively address the thoughts, feelings and actions of potential visitors.

Matt, 38 years old, is currently married with two sons, 15 and 13 years old respectively. His family has been wanting to experience theme parks in Japan but Matt is overwhelmed with the amount of information relating to Japanese theme parks. He has difficulty finding comprehensive information on family-friendly theme parks. Even with help and recommendation from his family and friends, it did not help him to narrow down his choices. On top of that, there are time constraints due to his work and his children’s school schedules, which means efficient planning is required.

By creating the persona, it helps me to better understand and cater to the needs and preferences of my target audience. Combining both empathy map and persona provides a more comprehensive understanding of my target audience, enabling me to design a more user-centric solutions.

Ideate

Moving on to the ideation phase, I research on existing websites to take reference from and learn from their best practices and develop my range of ideas to address the problem. I then select the most promising one to move forward with it.

Prototype

I then develop a low-fidelity prototype, where I create sketches of my microsite to visualise the chosen idea.

A sample flowchart is created to visualise the journey a user will have to take to complete a certain action. A few heuristic factors were kept in mind as the flowchart was designed, which include:

Error prevention: To eliminate error-prone conditions and provide users with a confirmation option before they submit the confirmation email

User control and freedom: A clearly marked emergency exit at the top left so that the user can easily back out of a process or undo an action.
